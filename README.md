# Agricultural-Rover
This autonomous farm rover uses a Raspberry Pi 5 brain and Arduino spine to navigate crop rows. It features 4WD tank drive for rough terrain, ultrasonic eyes for obstacle avoidance, and PIR sensors for safety. A dual-power 12V/5V system ensures reliability for AI-driven agricultural scouting.
To use my Rover, you need at least 1 plant or a field. You also need to print and stick numbers 1-4 on each side of A4-size paper, font size 700 (Google Docs). You can plug your phone into the USB port, and after that, you can enter your farm/field area using Google Maps, and when you're done, enter it and disconnect when prompted to. then the rover will automatically find the gaps between the rows of crops, once it does it will proceed to go inside the field and start scanning 360 degrees, if it detects something unusual like an pest or a disease it will judge its location based on the numbers on every side, this is accurate to centimeters, and then when its done it comes back to the location it started at and you can plug in your phone again and retrive all the data, you might think this is too time consuming, but at the cost of losing crops it is a very good solution. I made this because I wanted to try and make something that costs very little to manufacture, so farmers don't have to buy expensive rovers costing over atleast $10,000. My goal is to provide farmers with a cost-effective option.
